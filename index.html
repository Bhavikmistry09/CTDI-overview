Intro

CTDI Europe is a subsidiary of Communications Test Design, Inc. (CTDI). Founded in 1975, CTDI is a full-service, global engineering, repair and logistics company providing best-cost solutions to the communications industry. CTDI's customers include the major wireline and wireless telecom carriers, cable service providers, and major OEMs from around the world. CTDI maintains its corporate headquarters in West Chester, PA, and supports an expanding customer base with more than 14,000 employees in over 90 facilities worldwide.Take a sip of our work.

CTDI Europe operates the following divisions: Networks, Network Deployment Services, Set-Top Boxes, Mobile & Consumer Electronics and B2B. Our customers are present throughout the globe including EMEA, North America, LATAM and APAC. We serve our customers locally, enabling us to offer the best possible service and turnaround times due to our in-region presence. CTDI Europe operates 22 facilities in 15 countries.

Work

CTDI Europe employs its own IT experts. They maintain and enhance our IT systems, ensuring that our customers are served smoothly all across Europe and the whole world. CTDI Europe uses a huge number of different network technologies, depending on which application is required. Our systems are equipped with interfaces to numerous different customer systems, in line with the customers’ requirements. 24/7 Technical support We are committed to providing high-quality technical support to the end customer. 24 hours a day. 7 days a week. Our technical support is available around the clock: during business hours, via our in-house call center, and at night, via our standby service team.

CTDI’s robust and customizable warehouse management system, OLP+, captures all critical asset data to the serial level and provides visibility to all inventories and reporting via ETOS, our web-based business intelligence application. ETOS enables our customers complete visibility to all order and inventory activity via customizable, web-based, reporting.

About

The European Headquarters of CTDI is located in Malsch near Karlsruhe. The building was built in 1966 – back then, as the telecommunications supply and repair center of the former Deutsche Bundespost (whose telecommunications business was later privatized as Deutsche Telekom). But this has long since become a thing of the past. What has emerged on this property in the meantime is a modern campus: A repair center, a logistics and fulfillment center, a development center, and an administrative building.
